Technology

Youth technology training

We believe every child should have the opportunity to learn an object-oriented programming language regardless of background or socioeconomic status. Although Wisconsin produces more STEM graduates than anywhere else in the world, we have very few high school students pursuing web and software development. We intend to change that!

Learn to program the

Arduino is an open-source computer hardware and software company, project and user community for building digital devices and interactive objects that can sense and control the physical world.

Week 1

On Day 1, students are provided an Arduino Prototyping Kit as part of their tuition. They become familiar with the circuit board and all the wires and gadgets included. They also set up their working environment where they begin writing simple scripts to control lights, fans and many more toys!

Week 2

Building from week 1, week 2 dives deeper into the C-language by which the Arduino is built on. This object-oriented language is the basis for learning machine programming and artificial intelligence in many of today's most advanced robotics and "Internet of things" connected devices.

Week 3

Finally in week 3, student put their skills to the test and build a project as provided by the teaching team. Each student will build their own project but they will work in teams to do so. At the end of the program students will take their kits home with them and start programming anything that is not controlled by an Arduino!
