Entrepreneurship

Mentor-Driven Seed Accelerators

We have founded and led the development of numerous mentor-driven seed accelerators, innovation centers and innovation practices. Our work has been featured by the White House, The British Government, Federal and State of Wisconsin Department of Veterans Affairs, the National Science Foundation iCorps, and Wisconsin Housing and Economic Development Authority, to name a few.

Learn more about the entrepreneurial programs we've help start, launch and operate

Victory Spark

Established: 2012

Victory Spark is the preeminent mentor-driven seed accelerator dedicated to Veteran-led startups. As the first Global Accelerator Network in the State of Wisconsin and only Veteran focused program of the GAN, Victory Spark as funded and supported over 40 startups.


Lauanch Box Growth Accelerator

Established: 2014

The Launch Box Growth Accelerator is a 12-week “hands-on” program. The program is open to entrepreneurs, priority focus may be given to veteran, minority, women and other underrepresented-led start-ups. LaunchBox received its initial seed funding from the Small Busienss Administration's Growth Accelerator Grant program.


Revolution Labs

Established: 2013

We believe Central-Cities in America have tremendous potential to create innovative startups. Revolution Labs teachs the best methods for starting a business to entrepreneurs who live in America’s Central-Cities. We support the efforts of the entrepreneur with mentors and a world-class training program.


WERCBench Labs

Established: 2015

We're building the future of energy! WERCHBench Labs is focused on early-stage energy, power and control ventures with support from over 60 corporate mentors and service providers. WERCHBench was born out of the Midwest Energy Research Consortium in Milwaukee, WI and resides within the Energy Innovation Center. A full suite of services, equipment and ammenities are provided to help our startups grow quickly.


Vetransfer

Established: 2010

Founded through a grant by the Federal Department of Veterans Affairs Innovation Initiative (now Center for Innovation), Vetransfer streamlined the process by which disabled Veterans could pursue an entrepreneurial endevour while enlisted in Vocational Rehabilitation and Counseling program. Vetransfer founders were recognized by President Obama in 2012 as Champions of Change. Vetransfer's legecy continues through GEC's Victory Spark program.


94 Labs

Established: 2011

94 Labs was the first seed accelerator in Wisconsin. Over the course of 12 months, it funded and launched 26 software companies. Today, many of these startups and entrepreneurs have raised millions of dollars in funding. 94 Labs paved the way for 21st Entrepreneurship in Wisconsin - its legacy is continued today through the work of Gener8tor.
